## 2. Verified rules — 8 records (CS-PRE-001…008)

The doctrinal core, each verbatim-anchored:
- **Predecease → lapse:** sole/all nominees predecease → nomination **void** (ACT s.4(2)); heirs of predeceased nominee take **nothing**; case falls to Topic 2.
- **Partial predecease → redistribution:** deceased nominee's share to **surviving nominees pro-rata** (GSPR 15(4)); equal shares if none specified (15(3)); payment to survivors (ACT s.4A(3)).
- **Death after depositor, before settlement → vesting:** claim settled in favour of the **legal heirs of the last deceased nominee**, not the depositor's heirs (R60(2)(iv); identical legacy Vol I 87(2)(iii)) — expressly covers the nominee-died-after-claim-submission case.
- **Documents:** original death proof of every deceased nominee (GSPR 15(2); R60(2)(iii); Form 11 item 2); survivors' ID (15(7)); affidavit/disclaimer/indemnity only when the **nominee's heirs** proceed without legal evidence.
- **Fresh nomination duty** after death of all nominees (GSPR 14(6)(i)/(7)).
- **Minor-nominee side:** appointed-payee death → guardian payment (ACT s.4A(2)(b)).
- **Scheme-wise:** doctrine uniform across all nine schemes; SCSS spouse-continuation lapses with the spouse-nominee; MIS joint-holder death is survivorship, not nomination.

## 4. Conflicting rules

New entry **C-10:** the Act/GSPR are silent on the share of a co-nominee who dies **after** the depositor while other nominees survive; ACT s.4A(3) pays survivors without distinguishing pre/post-depositor deaths, while R60(2)(iv)'s vesting sentence applies only to the sole/last-surviving nominee. **Resolution implemented:** survivors take unless *no* nominee is alive at settlement; then last deceased nominee's heirs take.
