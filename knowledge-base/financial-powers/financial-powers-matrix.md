# Financial Sanctioning Powers — Deceased Claim Settlement

**Status:** DRAFT compiled during Topic 1 (Nomination). Will be finalised and re-verified in Topic 6 (Financial Powers).
**Governing texts:** Rule 60(2)–(4) POSB(CBS) Manual / Rule 87 POSB Manual Vol I as revised by **SB Order 31/2020** (28.08.2020); limits revised by **SB Order 36/2020** (06.11.2020); GSPR **Rule 15(6)** (₹5 lakh statutory ceiling for discretionary settlement); Act **s.4A(4)** (limit "as may be prescribed").

## Statutory ceiling & court-order requirements

- Discretionary settlement without nomination/legal evidence is capped at **₹5,00,000** (GSPR 15(6)(i); R60(1)(c)) and allowed only **after 6 months** from death.
- **Above ₹5,00,000 with no nomination:** payment only against **probate / letters of administration / succession certificate issued by Court, or legal heir certificate from revenue authority ≥ Tahsildar** (GSPR 15(6)(ii) as amended by G.S.R. 488(E) dt. 03.07.2023).
  - ⚠️ SB Order 36/2020 Note 2 (earlier) says only "Succession Certificate" — see conflicts register C-2; the later, higher instrument (amended GSPR) prevails, but field instructions have not been re-issued (open question).
- **Any dispute raised before payment:** settlement only on **court-issued Succession Certificate**, both slabs (GSPR 15(6) provisos, 2023).
- **Nomination or legal evidence present:** **no monetary ceiling** and **no court order** needed at any level.

## Operating rules attached to the powers

- Limits are applied per the **status of the post office** where the account stands (36/2020 Note 1).
- "Balance" for determining authority: credit at death **plus interest accrued up to end of preceding financial year** (accounts); **maturity value** (face + accrued interest to last completed year/half-year before death) for certificates (R60(4)(B) Note 2; R60(3) Explanation).
- Powers beyond **₹10,000** are exercised **personally** by the named officers (R60(4)(C) Note 1).
- Timelines: acknowledgment same day; sanction within **1 working day (nomination)** / **7 working days (all other cases)**; Divisional Office 7 working days (36/2020 Notes; 01/2023; R60(13)).
- The limit applies **per account / per certificate registration number** (R60(1) Note 1).
