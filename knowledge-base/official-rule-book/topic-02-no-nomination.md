# Topic 2 — No Nomination (Official Rule Book)

**Status:** ✅ **LOCKED — ClaimSahayak Official Rule Book, Version 1.0, Topic 2 – No Nomination** (approved 10.07.2026). Changes only via a new approved amendment entry; verification report retained at `verification-report/topic-02-no-nomination-verification.md`.
**Scope:** Settlement of the deceased depositor's balance when **no valid nomination is in force** at death (never made, cancelled under GSPR 14(6), void because all nominees predeceased the depositor, or account opened for a minor/person of unsound mind before 01.04.2018). Covers legal heirs, eligible claimants, affidavit/disclaimer/indemnity route, succession certificate/probate/letters of administration/legal-heir certificate route, monetary limits, forms, documents, scheme-wise differences.

**Legend:** ACT = Government Savings Promotion Act, 1873 (as amended w.e.f. 01.04.2018) · GSPR = Government Savings Promotion General Rules, 2018 (G.S.R. 1003(E) dt. 05.10.2018), Rule 15(6) as substituted by G.S.R. 488(E) dt. 03.07.2023 (SB Order 16/2023) · R60 = Rule 60 POSB(CBS) Manual / Rule 87 POSB Manual Vol I as revised by SB Order 31/2020 dt. 28.08.2020; limits per SB Order 36/2020 dt. 06.11.2020 · All schemes = SB, RD, TD, MIS, SCSS, PPF, NSC, KVP, SSA + discontinued Schedule schemes.

## A. When is there "no nomination in force"? (gateway conditions)

### CS-NON-001 — Triggers that put a claim on the no-nomination track
- **Official Source:** ACT s.4(2); GSPR 14(6)–(7); GSPR 14(5) proviso; GSPR 10(3) proviso; R60(1)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger → Decision:** A claim is processed **without nomination** when any of the following holds at the time of death:
  1. No nomination was ever registered (despite GSPR 14(1)/(4) obligation to nominate — failure to nominate does not bar heirs' claims);
  2. Nomination became **void** because the sole nominee or **all** nominees predeceased the depositor (ACT s.4(2)) — *detailed treatment in Topic 3*;
  3. Nomination **stood cancelled** on transfer of the account as security (GSPR 14(6)(ii)/Form 7) or transfer of deposit (ACT s.4(4)) and no fresh nomination was made;
  4. Minor/unsound-mind depositor's account opened **before 01.04.2018** (nomination not permissible) — payment to **guardian** (GSPR 10(3)/14(5) proviso; ACT s.4A(3A)); *Topic: Minor cases*;
  5. Depositor's valid-but-unregistered nomination exists → **not** a no-nomination case: it may be registered posthumously with Divisional Superintendent approval (R60(2) Note 5, Topic 1 CS-NOM-020(12)).
- **Cross References:** CS-NOM-002, CS-NOM-009, CS-NOM-010
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NON-002 — Two settlement paths and the six-month gate
- **Official Source:** **GSPR 15(6)** (as amended 03.07.2023); ACT s.4A(4); R60(1), R60(4)(A)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger:** Death of depositor (single) / all depositors (joint), no nomination in force
- **Decision:**
  - **Path 1 — Legal evidence any time:** if the claimant produces **probate of will / letters of administration / succession certificate (Indian Succession Act, 1925) / legal heir certificate issued by revenue authority not below Tahsildar with jurisdiction**, the claim is payable **irrespective of amount** and **without waiting period** (R60(3): sanction even before expiry of the waiting period; detailed in Topic "Legal Evidence").
  - **Path 2 — Discretionary (no legal evidence):** only if such evidence is **not produced within six months from the date of death**, and only up to **₹5,00,000** eligible amount, on Form 11 + affidavit/disclaimer/indemnity route (CS-NON-003).
- **Court Requirement:** None for Path 2 ≤ ₹5L; court/revenue document needed above ₹5L (CS-NON-005) or on dispute (CS-NON-006)
- **Conflict:** ACT s.4A(4) says **three months**; GSPR/R60 prescribe **six months** — Rule Engine uses six; flagged as C-1 in the conflicts register.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

## B. Discretionary settlement without legal evidence (≤ ₹5 lakh)

### CS-NON-003 — Eligible amount ≤ ₹5,00,000: documents and forms
- **Official Source:** **GSPR 15(6)(i)** (as amended by G.S.R. 488(E), 03.07.2023); **R60(4)(A)** (SB Order 31/2020, enclosure p.6)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Applicable Scenario:** No nomination, no legal evidence produced within/at 6 months, eligible amount ≤ ₹5,00,000
- **Trigger:** Legal heir(s) submit claim after expiry of **six months** from death
- **Decision:** The Authorised Officer / specified authority **may, for reasons to be recorded in writing**, pay any person appearing to be the **rightful claimant** entitled to receive the amount or administer the estate.
- **Required Forms:** **Form 11** (claim; DoP operational version per SB Order 31/2020 with two witnesses), **Form 13** (Affidavit), **Form 14** (Letter of disclaimer), **Form 15** (Bond of Indemnity)
- **Required Documents:** (a) death certificate of account holder (original, or photocopy compared with original and endorsed); (b) passbook / deposit receipt / statement of account **in original**; (c) **identity proof of the legal heir** (added 03.07.2023); claimant + two witnesses' self-attested ID & address proofs (Addendum 16.09.2020; SB Order 01/2023)
- **Affidavit Required:** YES (Form 13) · **Disclaimer Required:** YES (Form 14, from non-claiming heirs) · **Indemnity Required:** YES (Form 15 with sureties)
- **Stamp duty:** Forms 13/14/15 on **non-judicial stamp paper of the value required by the Stamp Act of the respective State** (R60(4)(A) Note 2(ii)). *(Specific values like ₹200/₹500 circulating in field guides are state practice — NOT a DoP rule.)*
- **Financial Authority:** per matrix (CS-NON-007) · **Court Order Required:** No
- **Continuation Allowed:** Yes — same options as nominee claims (R60(10)/(11)): RD/TD/NSC/KVP continuation; SCSS spouse rule
- **Payment Mode:** Crossed cheque or credit to claimant's POSB/bank account only (R60(10)(iii))
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NON-004 — Who may claim: entitlement under personal law; guardianship certificate
- **Official Source:** **R60(4)(A) Note 1 & Note 2(iii)** (SB Order 31/2020); R60(6)(iii); R60(7)
- **Decision:**
  - The claim application **must be filled by the person entitled under the law**: **Hindu Succession Act, 1956 ss.8/15** (Hindus — s.8 male intestate, s.15 female intestate), **Mohammedan law** (Muslims), **Indian Succession Act, 1925** (Christians and others).
  - A **guardianship certificate** must be submitted on behalf of **minor relatives** of the deceased if the claimant is **not** their guardian under applicable law; where **natural guardians** claim for minors, payment is made **without** insisting on a guardianship certificate (R60(6)(iii)).
  - Payment on behalf of a minor requires the "**minor is alive** and money required for the minor" certificate; payment directly to a minor is **not** a valid discharge (R60(7); ACT s.5).
- **Cross References:** Topic on Minor cases; CS-NOM-023 (foreign-resident heirs — consular/apostille authentication; payment only to POA holder in India)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

## C. Above ₹5 lakh and disputed cases

### CS-NON-005 — Eligible amount > ₹5,00,000: legal evidence mandatory
- **Official Source:** **GSPR 15(6)(ii)** (as amended 03.07.2023); R60(1)(c); SB Order 36/2020 Note 2
- **Trigger:** No nomination; eligible amount above ₹5,00,000
- **Decision:** Payment **only** on submission of **probate of will / letters of administration / succession certificate issued by the Court**, **or legal heir certificate issued by revenue authority not below the rank of Tahsildar having jurisdiction**, together with: claim form; passbook/deposit receipt/statement in original; death certificate; **identity proof of the legal heir**.
- **Affidavit/Disclaimer/Indemnity:** Not required (legal evidence replaces them)
- **Court Order Required:** YES in substance — court-issued document (probate/LoA/succession certificate) **or** the revenue-authority legal-heir certificate (the only non-court alternative, added 03.07.2023)
- **Conflict C-2:** SB Order 36/2020 Note 2 (earlier) mentions only "Succession Certificate"; the amended GSPR (later, higher instrument) accepts all four evidences. GSPR prevails; DoP field-table re-issue awaited.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NON-006 — Dispute raised before payment: court succession certificate only
- **Official Source:** **GSPR 15(6) provisos to (i) and (ii)** (inserted 03.07.2023)
- **Trigger:** **Any dispute** is raised before the Accounts Office **before payment** of the claim (either slab)
- **Decision:** Amount shall be paid **only** on a **succession certificate granted under the Indian Succession Act, 1925 issued by the Court**, with claim form, original passbook/receipt/statement, death certificate and identity proof of the legal heir. (Probate/LoA/Tahsildar certificate do **not** suffice once a dispute is raised.)
- **Court Order Required:** YES — succession certificate only
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

## D. Sanctioning powers, scrutiny and process (no-nomination cases)

### CS-NON-007 — Monetary limits of sanctioning authorities (no nomination & no legal evidence)
- **Official Source:** **R60(4)(B)** as revised by **SB Order 36/2020 dt. 06.11.2020** (amending the limits set by SB Order 31/2020); GSPR 15(6)(i) ceiling
- **Decision (current limits, per account/registration number, after 6 months):**
  | Authority | Limit |
  |---|---|
  | SPM — Time Scale departmental SO / LSG PO | **₹50,000** (above → Divisional Office) |
  | SPM/Dy PM/PM — HSG non-gazetted SOs & HPOs | **₹1,00,000** (above → Divisional Office) |
  | Sr. PM / Dy. Chief PM / SPOs / Dy. SPOs (Gaz. Gp-B); Chief PM GPO / SSPOs (Gaz. Gp-A) & equivalents | **₹5,00,000** |
  | Divisional Head | within ₹5,00,000 for cases forwarded; beyond own limit → competent authority |
  | > ₹5,00,000 | No discretionary power at any level — legal evidence route only (CS-NON-005) |
- **Ancillary rules:** limit determined by the **status of the post office** where the account stands (36/2020 Note 1); powers **beyond ₹10,000 exercised personally** (R60(4)(C) Note 1); "balance" = credit at death + interest accrued to end of preceding FY (accounts) / maturity value (certificates) (R60(4)(B) Note 2); **special-feature cases/lacunae → Directorate** (R60(4)(C)); historic limits retained in `financial-powers/financial-powers-matrix.md`.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (final re-verification scheduled in Financial Powers topic)

### CS-NON-008 — Scrutiny checklist and immunity of the sanctioning authority
- **Official Source:** **R60(4)(D)–(E), R60(6)** (SB Order 31/2020); ACT ss.4A(4), 5, 6, 7
- **Decision:**
  - Sanctioning authority settles **without reference to higher authority** unless the claim is **doubtful or contested** (then: inquire, record results, forward upward).
  - Checklist: account particulars verified by Postmaster; **witnesses accepted** on the claim; claimant ID + address proof attached; original/attested death certificate in order; **annexures (13/14/15) duly attested** by Notary/Oath Commissioner; **pledge released** (if pledged, pledgee is paid first to the extent of the claim).
  - If defects: address the **claimant directly** with guidance. If witnesses accepted by Director/CPM/Sr.PM/PM/SPM + ID/address + death proof + attested annexures present → **no further verification** (reiterated by SB Order 01/2023: no PRI(P)/SDI(P) inquiry).
  - **Discretion & immunity:** ACT ss.4A & 5 + GSPR 15(6)(i) give the sanctioning authority **full discretion** to pay whoever appears entitled, and **immunity for bona fide wrong payment**; ACT s.6 permits taking **security** for due administration; ACT s.7 allows **evidence on oath** (false statement = offence under IPC s.193).
  - Executors/administrators/creditors of the estate retain recovery rights **against the recipient**, not the Post Office (ACT s.5).
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NON-009 — Process mechanics common to no-nomination claims
- **Official Source:** R60(2)(vii) (mutatis mutandis via prescribed form), R60(4), R60(12)–(15); Addendum 16.09.2020; SB Order 01/2023; SB Order 36/2020 Notes
- **Decision:**
  - Claim submitted at **any post office** must be accepted (witnesses accepted there) and forwarded by **Service Insured Post the same day** to the PO of account.
  - Same-day **acknowledgment**; entry in **Register of Deceased Claim Cases**; disposal within **7 working days** (Divisional Office also 7 working days).
  - **Two witnesses** on the claim form; physical presence not required with self-attested witness ID/address photocopies bearing signatures.
  - Sanction recorded **on the claim form** (Form 11 part II); **sanction memo** only when an administrative authority (e.g., Divisional Head) sanctions — memo valid **one year**; claimant may opt for **transfer of RD/TD/certificates to own name** instead of payment.
  - **Death-certificate issuers** and photocopy-comparison rule as in CS-NOM-021; **reconciliation certificate** for name mismatch (CS-NOM-022); **presumed death after 7 years** with indemnity (CS-NOM-023); foreign claimants per CS-NOM-023.
  - Payment by **crossed cheque or credit to account only**; claimant gives account details + acquittance at submission (01/2023 ¶3(c)).
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NON-010 — Court-fee relief and balance certificate for heirs seeking court documents
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.8** (as amended 2018)
- **Trigger:** Heir applies to court for probate/LoA/succession certificate covering POSB deposits
- **Decision:** Deposit up to the **prescribed limit** is **excluded in computing court fees**; the claimant must exhibit to the court a **certificate of the deposit balance signed by the Authorised Officer** of the Post Office, which the court receives as evidence of the amount. (Post Office must issue such balance certificates on request.)
- **Open question OQ-7:** the current "prescribed limit" under s.8 (pre-1985 figure was ₹3,000, then "prescribed") — no prescription found in GSPR 2018; needs DEA/DoP confirmation.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (duty to issue certificate); limit **UNRESOLVED**

## E. Scheme-wise differences (no-nomination claims)

### CS-NON-011 — Scheme-wise interest & continuation for legal heirs
- **Official Source:** **R60(10)–(11)** (SB Order 31/2020) — table applies to "Nominee(s)/Legal heir(s)"; scheme gazette rules prevail
- **Decision highlights (full table in `cbs-manual/rule-60-deceased-claim-settlement.md`):**
  - **SB:** closure only; interest to preceding month.
  - **RD/TD/NSC/KVP:** legal heir may **close immediately or continue till maturity** by transfer to own name (new AOF + KYC; CIF change in CBS). Premature-vs-continued interest per the 2019 scheme rules tables.
  - **MIS:** closure any time; interest to month preceding refund; MIS-specific note — on death of a **joint holder**, if deposits then exceed the single-holder ceiling, excess is refunded and interest adjusted (SB Order 29/2021 + Addendum, Finacle HINTTM/HCAACTD procedure).
  - **SCSS:** the **spouse-continuation option applies only where the spouse is sole nominee** (R60(10) text) — where there is **no nomination**, heirs close the account; SCSS rate to date of death, POSA rate thereafter. *(Cross-check against SCSS Scheme 2019 gazette planned in scheme-wise topic — the scheme rules also allow spouse continuation as legal heir; flagged OQ-8.)*
  - **PPF:** closure only (no continuation by heirs); PPF rate to end of month preceding payment; PPF balance **protected from attachment** (ACT s.14A) but payable to heirs per this Topic.
  - **SSA:** closure; SSA rate to date of death, POSA rate thereafter.
  - Claims of **discontinued schemes** sanctioned at HPO/GPO.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (as DoP gist; scheme-gazette cross-verification is a separate planned topic)
