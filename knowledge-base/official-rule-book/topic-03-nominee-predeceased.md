# Topic 3 — Nominee Predeceased / Nominee Dies Before Settlement (Official Rule Book)

**Status:** ✅ **LOCKED — ClaimSahayak Official Rule Book, Version 1.0, Topic 3 – Nominee Predeceased** (approved 10.07.2026). Changes only via a new approved amendment entry; verification report retained at `verification-report/topic-03-nominee-predeceased-verification.md`.
**Scope:** Every official rule governing the death of a registered nominee — (1) before the depositor, (2) after the depositor but before settlement, (3) one or more of several nominees dying, (4) nominee dying after submitting the claim but before payment, (5) effect on surviving nominees, (6) whether the deceased nominee's heirs inherit or the nomination lapses.

**Legend:** as in Topics 1–2. Key doctrinal split verified across all layers:
> **Nominee dies BEFORE the depositor** → that nominee's interest **lapses**; share redistributes to surviving nominees; if none survive, the **nomination is void** and the case becomes a Topic-2 (No Nomination) claim by the **depositor's** heirs.
> **Nominee survives the depositor but dies before settlement/payment** → the nominee's entitlement has **vested**; the claim is settled in favour of the **legal heirs of the (last deceased) nominee**, *not* the depositor's heirs.

---

## A. Nominee dies before the depositor

### CS-PRE-001 — Sole nominee (or all nominees) predecease the depositor → nomination void
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.4(2)**: "Any nomination … shall become void if the nominee predeceases, or where there are two or more nominees, all the nominees predecease, the depositor." (India Code PDF p.4)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger:** Death of sole nominee, or of all nominees, during depositor's lifetime
- **Decision:** Nomination **becomes void by operation of law**. On the depositor's later death the account has **no nomination in force** → settle under **Topic 2** (GSPR 15(6); R60(3)/(4)): legal-evidence route any amount, or discretionary route ≤ ₹5,00,000 after six months with Forms 13/14/15.
- **Effect on heirs of the predeceased nominee:** **None — the nomination lapses**; the predeceased nominee's heirs have **no claim** through the nomination. (Statutory consequence of s.4(2); reinforced by GSPR 15(3)-(4) which route shares only to *surviving* nominees.)
- **Required Forms/Documents:** those of Topic 2 (CS-NON-003/005); death certificates of depositor **and** nominee(s) establish the sequence of deaths
- **Financial Authority / Court:** per Topic 2 matrix
- **Cross References:** CS-NOM-002; CS-NON-001(2); CS-PRE-006 (depositor's duty to make fresh nomination)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-PRE-002 — One (or some) of several nominees predecease the depositor → share redistributes
- **Official Source:** **GSPR 15(3)–(4)**; **ACT s.4A(3)**; **R60(2)(iv)–(v)** (SB Order 31/2020); POSB Manual Vol I Rule 87(2)(iii) (legacy, same effect)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger:** Depositor dies; nomination in force; one or more nominees died before the depositor, at least one nominee surviving
- **Decision:**
  - Payment goes to the **surviving nominee(s)** (ACT s.4A(3)).
  - The deceased nominee's **specified share is distributed among the surviving nominees in the same proportion as their specified shares** (GSPR 15(4); R60(2)(v)).
  - If no shares were specified: surviving nominees take in **equal proportion** (GSPR 15(3); R60(2)(iv)).
- **Effect on heirs of the predeceased nominee:** none — share passes to surviving nominees, **not** to the predeceased nominee's heirs.
- **Required Documents:** proof of death of the depositor **and original proof of death of the deceased nominee(s)** with the claim (GSPR 15(2); R60(2)(iii); Form 11 enclosure item 2 "(***)"); ID documents of surviving nominees (GSPR 15(7)); witnesses per Addendum 16.09.2020
- **Required Forms:** Form 11 (strike-out conventions apply); no affidavit/disclaimer/indemnity — nomination is still in force
- **Financial Authority:** nomination track — **any SPM/PM, no limit** (R60(2) Note 1); 1-working-day timeline
- **Court Requirement:** None
- **Cross References:** Topic 4 (Multiple Nominees) for share mechanics, joint/separate claims, minors among nominees
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-PRE-003 — Trustee-capacity or appointed-payee complications *(boundary note)*
- **Official Source:** GSPR 14(1)(c) (owner vs trustee), 14(2) (appointee for minor nominee)
- **Decision (only what official text supports):** The owner/trustee capacity and any appointed payee attach to the **named nominee**. Where that nominee predeceases the depositor, the nomination-borne arrangement lapses with the nominee (CS-PRE-001/002); no official text transfers a trustee-nominee's role to another person.
- **Open question OQ-10:** no official instrument located on substitution of a deceased **trustee**-nominee or a deceased **appointed payee** (14(2)) during the depositor's lifetime other than the depositor's own fresh nomination (CS-PRE-006).
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (as boundary); gap logged

## B. Nominee survives the depositor but dies before settlement

### CS-PRE-004 — Vesting rule: settle in favour of the legal heirs of the (last deceased) nominee
- **Official Source:** **R60(2)(iv)** (SB Order 31/2020, enclosure p.3): "On the death of the last surviving nominee or the sole nominee, the claim in respect of the account will be settled **in favour of the legal heir of the last deceased nominee and not in favour of the LEGAL heir of the deceased depositor**." Identically in **POSB Manual Vol I Rule 87(2)(iii)** (legacy anchor, India Code edition p.125)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Applicable Scenario:** Depositor died with nomination in force; the sole nominee — or, where several, the **last surviving** nominee — dies **after the depositor** but **before the claim is settled/paid** (this includes a nominee who dies after submitting the claim but before payment — the entitlement vested at the depositor's death, and the rule text keys on settlement "on the death of the last surviving nominee or the sole nominee", without carving out pending claims)
- **Trigger:** Death of sole/last-surviving nominee post-depositor, pre-payment
- **Decision:** Claim settled in favour of the **legal heirs of the last deceased nominee**. The depositor's own legal heirs have **no claim**.
- **Required Documents:** death certificates of the **depositor and of the nominee** (originals / compared photocopies); passbook/certificates; claimant (nominee's heir) ID + address proof; **proof of heirship to the nominee** — per the general evidence framework: probate/LoA/succession certificate/Tahsildar legal-heir certificate, or (≤ ₹5,00,000, after the waiting period) the affidavit/disclaimer/indemnity route of GSPR 15(6)/R60(4) applied to the **nominee's** estate
- **Affidavit/Disclaimer/Indemnity:** required only when the nominee's heirs proceed without legal evidence (Forms 13/14/15 relate to heirship of the **nominee**)
- **Financial Authority (engine guidance + flag):** R60 does not prescribe a distinct sanctioning column for this hybrid case. The claim arrives **without a living nominee and rests on heirship evidence**, so the sanctioning powers follow the evidence produced — legal-evidence column (no limit at HSG-I/HO/GPO; Divisional Head beyond SPM limits) or no-evidence column (₹50k/₹1L/₹5L, 6 months) as applicable. Logged as **open question OQ-11** for Directorate confirmation.
- **Court Requirement:** only per evidence route chosen / dispute proviso (CS-NON-006)
- **Cross References:** CS-NON-003/005/006/007; Topic 4
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (vesting rule verbatim); sanctioning-column mapping flagged OQ-11

### CS-PRE-005 — Some of several nominees die after the depositor, others survive
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.4A(3)** ("Where a deposit is payable to two or more nominees **and either or any of them is dead**, the deposit shall be paid to the surviving nominee or nominees"); GSPR 15(3)–(4); R60(2)(iv)–(v), (xi)
- **Trigger:** Depositor died; between death and settlement, one or more (but not all) nominees die
- **Decision:** Payment to the **surviving nominee(s)**. Statute directs the *deposit* to survivors regardless of whether a co-nominee died before or after the depositor — the Act does not distinguish; GSPR 15(4)'s redistribution language likewise keys on "if any nominee dies".
- **Doctrinal tension (flagged, not resolved by any official text):** for a nominee who died **after** the depositor, R60(2)(iv)'s vesting sentence applies expressly only when the **sole/last-surviving** nominee dies. Read together, the official texts give survivors the whole deposit unless **no** nominee remains alive at settlement, in which case the last deceased nominee's heirs take (CS-PRE-004). Rule Engine should implement exactly this; recorded as **conflict C-10** (statute/GSPR silent on post-depositor vesting of a deceased co-nominee's share where other nominees survive) with the resolution above.
- **Required Documents:** original death proofs of every deceased nominee (R60(2)(iii)); survivors' ID (15(7))
- **Financial Authority:** nomination track — no limit, 1 working day
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (payment-to-survivors); tension logged as C-10
- **Cross References:** Topic 4 (Multiple Nominees) — shares, joint/separate claims, partial payment with notice (legacy Vol I 87(2)(v)) vs current R60(2)(xi) all-nominees payment

## C. Consequences during the depositor's lifetime

### CS-PRE-006 — Depositor must make a fresh nomination after all nominees die
- **Official Source:** **GSPR 14(6)(i)** (nomination stands cancelled on "death of all the nominee") + **14(7)** (fresh nomination "shall be required")
- **Trigger:** All nominees die during depositor's lifetime
- **Decision:** Nomination stands cancelled; depositor **shall make a fresh nomination** (Form 10, with passbook; witnesses only if illiterate depositor). Failure leaves the account on the no-nomination track at death.
- **Fee:** change/cancellation fee ₹50 per Schedule II(b) — reported abolished w.e.f. 02.04.2025 (CS-NOM-024, still PROVISIONAL)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-PRE-007 — Death of the appointed payee or guardian relationships (minor-nominee side)
- **Official Source:** GSPR 14(2); ACT s.4A(2); R60(2)(vi), R60(7); GSPR 10(4) as amended by G.S.R. 488(E) (guardian succession on death of guardian / court order)
- **Decision:** If the **minor nominee** survives but the **appointed payee** has died (or none was appointed), payment on the depositor's death goes to the **guardian of the minor nominee** (ACT s.4A(2)(b); GSPR 15(5); R60(2)(vi)); natural guardians need no guardianship certificate (R60(6)(iii)); payment directly to the minor is not a valid discharge (R60(7) Note 2).
- **Cross References:** Minor Nominee topic (future) for full treatment
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

## D. Scheme-wise differences

### CS-PRE-008 — Scheme-wise notes for nominee-death scenarios
- **Official Source:** R60(10)–(11) (SB Order 31/2020); SB Order 29/2021 + Addendum (official); scheme rules 2019 (gazette cross-check pending — scheme-wise topic)
- **Decisions:**
  - **Continuation options** (RD/TD/NSC/KVP transfer-to-own-name; SCSS spouse rule) are exercised by the person **entitled at settlement**: surviving nominee(s), or — under CS-PRE-004 — the last deceased nominee's legal heirs.
  - **SCSS:** spouse-continuation exists only where the **spouse is the sole nominee**; if that spouse-nominee dies before exercising it, the option lapses — settlement to the spouse-nominee's heirs under CS-PRE-004; SCSS interest to date of the **depositor's** death, POSA rate thereafter (R60(10) item 5).
  - **MIS joint accounts:** death of a **joint holder** is a survivorship event, not a nominee event; nominee rights arise only on death of **all** holders (GSPR 14(1)); excess-deposit interest adjustment per SB Order 29/2021.
  - **No scheme-specific deviation** from the void/vesting doctrine was found in any official instrument for SB/RD/TD/MIS/SCSS/PPF/NSC/KVP/SSA — the Act/GSPR/R60 rules above apply uniformly (scheme gazettes to be re-checked in the scheme-wise topic; OQ-12).
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (uniform application; gazette re-check logged)
