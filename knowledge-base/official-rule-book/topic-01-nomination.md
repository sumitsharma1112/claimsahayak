# Topic 1 — Nomination (Official Rule Book)

**Status:** ✅ **LOCKED — ClaimSahayak Official Rule Book, Version 1.0, Topic 1 – Nomination** (approved 10.07.2026). Changes only via a new approved amendment entry; verification report retained at `verification-report/topic-01-nomination-verification.md`.
**Scope:** Making, varying, cancelling a nomination; legal effect of nomination; payment to nominee(s) on death of depositor. Deep procedure for multiple nominees, minor nominees, no-nomination and legal-evidence cases is cross-referenced and will be elaborated in Topics 2–5.

**Legend:** ACT = Government Savings Promotion Act, 1873 (as amended by Finance Act 2018, w.e.f. 01.04.2018) · GSPR = Government Savings Promotion General Rules, 2018 (G.S.R. 1003(E) dt. 05.10.2018) · R60 = Revised Rule 60 of POSB (CBS) Manual / Rule 87 of POSB Manual Vol I as substituted by SB Order 31/2020 dt. 28.08.2020. All schemes = POSA(SB), RD, TD, MIS, SCSS, PPF, NSC, KVP, SSA (and discontinued NSS-Schedule schemes).

## A. Statutory layer (Act of 1873, as amended w.e.f. 01.04.2018)

### CS-NOM-001 — Nomination is mandatory and may name one or more individuals
- **Official Source:** Government Savings Promotion Act, 1873, **Section 4(1)** (substituted by Finance Act 2018, s.119)
- **Document:** India Code consolidated Act PDF (`a1873-05.pdf`), p.3
- **Effective Date:** 01.04.2018 · **Superseded By:** — (in force)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Applicable Situation / Trigger:** Opening/holding any account under a Savings Scheme
- **Decision:** Depositor(s) **shall designate one or more individuals** as nominee(s), entitled on death of the depositor of a single account or **all** depositors of a joint account, to receive the sum due **as owner or as trustee**, to the extent specified at the time of nomination. If the depositor is a minor or person of unsound mind, the **guardian** designates the nominee.
- **Required Forms:** Form 1 (at opening) / Form 10 (later) per GSPR
- **Court Requirement:** None
- **Exceptions:** Armed-forces requisition overrides nomination (CS-NOM-017)
- **Cross References:** CS-NOM-005 (GSPR cap of 4), CS-NOM-010
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**
- **Notes:** The Act does not cap the number of nominees; the cap of four is imposed by GSPR Rule 14(1).

### CS-NOM-002 — Nomination becomes void if all nominees predecease the depositor
- **Official Source:** ACT **Section 4(2)**; ACT **Section 4A(3)** (survivorship among nominees)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger:** Death of nominee(s) before depositor
- **Decision:** Nomination is void if the sole nominee — or, where several, **all** nominees — predecease the depositor. Where the deposit is payable to two or more nominees and any of them is dead **at the time of payment**, payment goes to the surviving nominee(s).
- **Cross References:** GSPR 14(6) (CS-NOM-009), GSPR 15(4) (CS-NOM-013), R60(2)(iv)-(v) (CS-NOM-020); Topic 2 (Multiple Nominees)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-003 — Appointee to receive payment during nominee's minority
- **Official Source:** ACT **Section 4(3)**; ACT **Section 4A(2)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger:** Nominee is a minor
- **Decision:** Depositor may appoint, in the prescribed manner, a person to receive the deposit if the depositor dies during the nominee's minority. On death: payment to the appointed person; if none, to the **guardian of the minor** for the use of the minor.
- **Required Forms:** Form 1 ¶17 / Form 10 (appointment block)
- **Cross References:** GSPR 14(2) (CS-NOM-006), GSPR 15(5), R60(2)(vi), R60(7); Topic 3 (Minor Nominee)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-004 — Transfer of deposit cancels nomination; statutory discharge; guardian payment for pre-2018 minor accounts
- **Official Source:** ACT **Section 4(4)** (ins. 2018), **Section 4A(1)** (payment to nominee), **Section 4A(3A)** (ins. 2018), **Section 5** (discharge), **Section 6** (security), **Section 7** (oath/penalty), **Section 8** (court-fee exclusion)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Decisions:**
  - 4(4): Transfer of deposit, if permitted under a scheme, **automatically cancels** a nomination previously made.
  - 4A(1): If a nomination is in force at death, the deposit **shall be paid to the nominee**.
  - 4A(3A): Deposit of a minor/person of unsound mind who died **without nominee before 01.04.2018** → paid to the **guardian**.
  - 5: Payment per the Act is a **full discharge**; executor/administrator and creditors retain recovery rights against the recipient (not against the Post Office).
  - 6: Authorised Officer **may take security** (basis for indemnity) from any person paid under s.4A(4).
  - 7: Authorised Officer may take **evidence on oath**; false statements punishable under IPC s.193.
  - 8: Deposit up to the prescribed limit excluded when computing **court fees** on probate/LoA/succession certificate; Post Office issues a balance certificate for the court.
- **Cross References:** GSPR 16 (pledge) — CS-NOM-009; Topics 4–5
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**
- **Notes (conflict):** s.4A(4) uses a **three-month** window before discretionary settlement without legal evidence; GSPR 15(6) and R60(4) prescribe **six months**. See `verification-report/topic-01` Conflict C-1.

## B. GSPR 2018 layer (statutory rules)

### CS-NOM-005 — Making a nomination: up to four individuals, shares, owner/trustee capacity
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 14(1)**, gazette p.36 (PDF p.5)
- **SB Order No.:** — (gazette) · **Effective:** 12.10.2018 (publication) · **Superseded By:** — (in force)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Trigger:** Opening of account (single or joint)
- **Decision:** Depositor(s) **shall nominate 1–4 individuals** who, on death of the depositor (single) or **all** depositors (joint), are entitled to the eligible balance. Nomination is made **at the time of opening** by furnishing in **Form 10** (nomination section of Form 1 in practice): (a) name(s) of nominee(s); (b) **percentage share** of each; (c) whether each nominee receives as **beneficiary (absolute owner)** or as **trustee** for the depositor's legal heirs.
- **Required Forms:** Form 1 ¶17 (at opening) / Form 10
- **Required Documents:** Passbook (for post-opening nomination)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**
- **Notes:** "Eligible balance" = credit balance incl. accrued interest minus recoveries for outstanding loan principal/interest (GSPR 3(1)(c)).

### CS-NOM-006 — Minor nominee: appointment of payee in Form 10
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 14(2)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Trigger:** Any nominee is a minor
- **Decision:** While nominating, the depositor **shall appoint an individual** (particulars in Form 10) to receive payment of the eligible balance if the depositor dies during the nominee's minority.
- **Cross References:** CS-NOM-003; Topic 3
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-007 — Variation/cancellation of nomination any time before maturity
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 14(3)**; Schedule II item (b) for fee
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Trigger:** Depositor wishes to change or cancel nomination
- **Decision:** Nomination may be **varied by fresh application in Form 10** together with the **passbook**, at any time **before maturity** of the account.
- **Required Forms:** Form 10 · **Required Documents:** Passbook
- **Fee:** ₹50 per Schedule II(b) (2018). **Deleted w.e.f. 02.04.2025 by G.S.R. 214(E)** — see CS-NOM-024 (PROVISIONAL).
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (base rule)

### CS-NOM-008 — Pre-01.04.2018 accounts without nomination: depositor must nominate
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 14(4)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Trigger:** Account opened before 01.04.2018 without nomination (except minor/unsound-mind accounts)
- **Decision:** Depositor **shall nominate immediately**, and in any case **before maturity**.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-009 — Guardian nominates for minor/unsound-mind accounts; cancellation events; witnesses for illiterate depositor
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 14(5), 14(6), 14(7), 14(8)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Decisions:**
  - 14(5): For accounts of/for a minor or person of unsound mind, **the guardian makes the nomination and may nominate any individual including himself**. For such accounts opened **before 01.04.2018**, **no nomination is allowed**; on death of the depositor the eligible amount is paid to the **guardian**.
  - 14(6): Nomination **stands cancelled** on (i) **death of all nominees**, or (ii) **transfer of the account as security** under Rule 16 (pledge).
  - 14(7): A **fresh nomination is required** after either event in 14(6).
  - 14(8): For an **illiterate depositor**, the thumb impression on a nomination (making, altering, fresh) must be **attested by two witnesses**. **No witness is required for a literate depositor** for making/altering nomination.
- **Cross References:** GSPR 10(3) (CS-NOM-010); Form 7 (pledge) auto-cancels nomination (form text: "Nomination vide registration number … stands cancelled")
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-010 — Death of minor/unsound-mind depositor: balance to nominee (guardian for pre-2018 accounts)
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 10(3)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Trigger:** Death of minor or person of unsound mind who is the depositor
- **Decision:** Eligible balance is paid **to the nominee**; for such accounts opened before 01.04.2018 (nomination not permissible), to the **guardian**.
- **Cross References:** R60(1) Note 2 (claim + death certificate of minor); Topic 3
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-011 — NRI may be a nominee (non-repatriation basis)
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 14(9)**, inserted by **G.S.R. 488(E) dt. 03.07.2023** (Government Savings Promotion General (Second Amendment) Rules, 2023), circulated by **SB Order 16/2023 dt. 18.07.2023**
- **Effective:** 06.07.2023 (gazette publication) · **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Decision:** Non-resident Indians are **eligible to be nominated** as nominees, subject to payment being on a **non-repatriation basis**.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-012 — Payment to nominee on death: claim in Form 11 with proof of death
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 15(1), 15(2)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Trigger:** Death of depositor (single) / all depositors (joint) with nomination in force
- **Decision:** Nominee applies in **Form 11** to the Accounts Office with **proof of death of the depositor**, and, if any other nominee has died, **proof of death of that nominee**.
- **Required Forms:** Form 11 · **Required Documents:** Death certificate(s), passbook (per Form 11 enclosure list)
- **Financial Authority:** Any SPM/PM where nomination is registered — no monetary limit (R60(2) Note 1)
- **Court Requirement:** None
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-013 — Shares among surviving nominees
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 15(3), 15(4)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Trigger:** Two or more surviving nominees / a nominee has died
- **Decision:** Payment in the **proportion specified** by the depositor; if none specified, **equal shares**. A deceased nominee's specified share is **distributed among surviving nominees pro-rata** to their specified shares.
- **Cross References:** Topic 2 (Multiple Nominees); R60(2)(iv)–(v) incl. last-surviving-nominee rule (CS-NOM-020)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-014 — Minor surviving nominee: payment to appointee, else guardian
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 15(5)**
- **Decision:** Where the surviving nominee is a minor, payment is made to the person appointed under Rule 14(2); if none, to the **guardian of the minor**.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** · **Cross References:** Topic 3

### CS-NOM-015 — Surviving nominees must furnish identity documents
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 15(7)**, inserted by **G.S.R. 488(E) dt. 03.07.2023** (SB Order 16/2023)
- **Decision:** Surviving nominees must submit any of the **identity documents prescribed in GSPR Rule 6** (Aadhaar; PAN/Form 60; officially valid documents).
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-016 — No nomination in force: settlement paths (summary; detail in Topics 4–5)
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 15(6)** as substituted by **G.S.R. 488(E) dt. 03.07.2023** (SB Order 16/2023). Original 15(6) of 2018: **VERIFIED-SUPERSEDED** (text preserved in `gspr/`)
- **Trigger:** Depositor dies, no nomination in force, and probate/LoA/succession certificate **or legal heir certificate issued by revenue authority not below Tahsildar** is not produced within **six months** of death
- **Decision (current text):**
  - ≤ ₹5,00,000 eligible amount: Authorised Officer/specified authority **may, for reasons recorded in writing**, pay the rightful claimant on **Form 11** + death certificate + original passbook/receipt/statement + **Affidavit Form 13** + **Disclaimer Form 14** + **Indemnity Bond Form 15** + **identity proof of legal heir**.
  - > ₹5,00,000: payment on **probate / letters of administration / succession certificate / Tahsildar-or-above legal heir certificate** + claim form + passbook + death certificate + ID proof of legal heir.
  - **Dispute raised before payment:** payment only on **succession certificate issued by court** (both slabs).
- **Court Requirement:** Only above ₹5 lakh without other accepted legal evidence, or in case of dispute
- **Cross References:** Topics 4 (No Nomination) & 5 (Legal Evidence); R60(4); financial-powers matrix
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-017 — Armed forces exception: requisition overrides nomination
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 17**
- **Trigger:** Depositor serving in Army/Air Force/Navy dies or deserts
- **Decision:** On requisition by the Commanding Officer or Committee of Adjustment under the Army & Air Force (Disposal of Private Property) Act 1950 / Navy Act 1957, the Accounts Office is **bound to pay the eligible amount to the CO/Committee even if a nomination is in force**.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-018 — Nomination registration number/date recorded in passbook; payment modes on closure
- **Official Source:** **GSPR Rule 18(1)(i)** (passbook contents incl. "Nomination registration number and date"); **GSPR Rule 19(2)** (payment at option of depositor/guardian/nominee/legal heir: credit to savings account of payee / crossed cheque / cash below Income-tax cash limit)
- **Cross References:** R60(10)(iii) restricts deceased-claim payment to **cheque or credit to savings account** (no cash) — see C-6 in conflicts register
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

## C. Departmental procedure layer (Rule 60 CBS Manual / Rule 87 Vol I, as revised by SB Order 31/2020; SB Orders 36/2020, 01/2023)

### CS-NOM-019 — Nominee entitled immediately, irrespective of amount, without legal evidence
- **Official Source:** **R60(2)(i)** (SB Order 31/2020 enclosure, p.3)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All (accounts & certificates, incl. discontinued schemes)
- **Trigger:** Registered nomination in force at time of death
- **Decision:** Nominee(s) entitled to the credit **immediately after death, irrespective of amount, without production of legal evidence**.
- **Financial Authority:** Postmasters of **all** SOs/HOs sanction irrespective of any limit where nomination is registered in their office (R60(2) Note 1). Discontinued-scheme claims sanctioned at the HPO (Note 2). GDS BPM accepts and forwards; Account Office sanctions (Note 3).
- **Timeline:** Settlement within **1 working day** where nomination exists (SB Order 36/2020 Note; reiterated SB Order 01/2023 ¶3(g)); acknowledgment issued same day (R60(12); 36/2020 Note 1)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-020 — Nomination-claim procedure (documents, verification, sanction on claim form)
- **Official Source:** **R60(2)(ii)–(xii)** (SB Order 31/2020); **Addendum to SB Order 31/2020 dt. 16.09.2020** (witnesses); **SB Order 01/2023 dt. 09.01.2023**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All
- **Key decisions:**
  1. Claim in **prescribed form in duplicate** at the PO where the account stands, with **original proof of death** and passbook/certificates. Photocopy of death certificate acceptable if compared with original and endorsed "Compared with original and found correct" (R60(2)(ii)).
  2. If a co-nominee has died: original proof of that nominee's death also required (R60(2)(iii)).
  3. Claim submitted at another PO: any GDS BPM/SPM/PM/Sr.PM/CPM/Director must **accept it, accept the witnesses, and forward by Service Insured Post the same day** to the PO of account (R60(2)(vii)).
  4. Postmaster verifies: depositor name (Finacle vs passbook vs death certificate); nomination details; balance; **no court order/tax attachment/freeze/pledge/objection pending**; nominee identified by **ID + address proof** (R60(2)(viii); GSPR 15(7)).
  5. Entry in **deceased claim/nomination register**; **sanction issued on the claim form itself** — no separate closure form; the receipted claim form is the **account closure voucher**; photocopy/duplicate kept as office copy (R60(2)(ix)–(x)).
  6. **No separate sanction memo** at HO/SO (discontinued w.e.f. 01.01.2011 by SB Order 25/2010; reiterated SB Order 01/2023 ¶2(a), ¶3(d) — SPM/PM sanctions on Form-11 second part "For office use only").
  7. **No PRI(P)/SDI(P) field verification** once documents are complete (SB Order 01/2023 ¶3(e)).
  8. **Witnesses:** two witnesses on the claim form; **physical presence not required** if self-attested photocopies of witness ID/address proof bearing the witness's signature are submitted (Addendum 16.09.2020 ¶2; list of 13 acceptable ID/address documents annexed — per KYC master circular SB Order 14/2012 and GSPR 2018). KYC documents of claimant verified against originals (SB Order 01/2023 ¶3(a)-(b)).
  9. All surviving nominees paid **per specified shares** after acquittance; in exceptional circumstances nominees may authorize one nominee via **disclaimer in GSPR Form 14 + their KYC documents** (R60(2)(xi) & Note).
  10. **Last surviving/sole nominee dies after depositor:** claim settled in favour of the **legal heir of the last deceased nominee**, not the legal heir of the depositor (R60(2)(iv); POSB Manual Vol I Rule 87(2)(iii) identical) → Topic 2.
  11. Lost passbook/certificate: nominee may obtain duplicate/passbook in own name **after** claim admitted & sanctioned (R60(2)(xii)).
  12. **Unregistered nomination:** if depositor's nomination was in order but the PO failed to register it, it **can be registered posthumously** after verification and approval of the **Divisional Superintendent** (R60(2) Note 5).
  13. **Nominee precedence:** nominee takes precedence over all other claimants; payment to nominee absolves the PO. **But** if a legal heir presents a court-issued Succession Certificate/Probate/LoA **before sanction** in favour of the nominee, the claim is settled in favour of the **court-document holder** (R60(2) Note 4).
  14. Claimant should give **bank/POSB account details at submission** with acquittance signature so payment is credited without a second visit (SB Order 01/2023 ¶3(c)).
- **Payment Mode:** **Crossed cheque or credit to claimant's savings/bank account only** (R60(10)(iii))
- **Continuation Allowed:** RD/TD/SCSS(spouse-sole-nominee)/NSC/KVP may be **continued/transferred to claimant's name till maturity** instead of closure (R60(10) table, R60(11); sanction memo text) → scheme-wise detail in later topic
- **Timeline:** 1 working day (nomination) / 7 working days (others); register reviewed monthly; inspecting officers check delays (R60(13), 36/2020, 01/2023)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-021 — Death certificate: acceptable issuers
- **Official Source:** **R60(5)** (SB Order 31/2020)
- **Decision:** Death certificate/proof in original from **Municipality/local authority/hospital/police station**; where none exist — from a **Gazetted Officer, MP, MLA, Panchayat Officer, Mukhiya/Village Police Patel**; balance ≤ ₹500 — last employer/attending doctor/hakim; **Parsee panchayat certificate / church burial certificate** acceptable where civil certificate unobtainable. Photocopy acceptable when compared with original and endorsed.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-022 — Reconciliation certificate when deceased's name differs across documents
- **Official Source:** **R60(15)** (SB Order 31/2020)
- **Trigger:** Name mismatch between account/certificate records and death certificate
- **Decision:** Claimant applies to **Divisional Head / Head of GPO / Gazetted HO** with a manuscript declaration, documentary evidence and **two witnesses with ID/address proof**; the authority issues a **Reconciliation Certificate** (format prescribed) which is attached to the claim.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-NOM-023 — Claims of depositors not heard of for 7 years; claimants residing abroad
- **Official Source:** **R60(8)** (presumed death) and **R60(9)** (foreign claimants) (SB Order 31/2020)
- **Decisions:**
  - Holder not heard of for **>7 years** treated as dead, provided disappearance is established and claimant **indemnifies** the Government.
  - Foreign-resident claimants: claim/disclaimer/death certificate/power of attorney executed abroad need **Indian consular authentication** unless the country is covered by Notaries Act 1952 reciprocal arrangement (UK, Hungary, Ireland, Belgium, New Zealand) or the **Hague Apostille Convention** (list in order). Payment made **only to a power-of-attorney holder in India**; no direct foreign remittance.
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**
