# Topic 5 — Minor Nominee (Official Rule Book)

**Status:** ✅ **LOCKED — ClaimSahayak Official Rule Book, Version 1.0, Topic 5 – Minor Nominee** (approved 10.07.2026). Changes only via a new approved amendment entry; verification report retained at `verification-report/topic-05-minor-nominee-verification.md`.
**Scope:** Every official rule where a **nominee is a minor** — sole minor nominee, minor among multiple nominees, all-minor nominees, representation by natural/court-appointed guardian, guardian's death, minor attaining majority, direct-payment validity, guardian documents, powers, scheme-wise differences.
**"Minor":** person who has not attained majority under the Indian Majority Act, 1875 (ACT s.3(i)) — i.e., under 18 years.
**Track note:** a claim with a registered nomination in favour of a minor is a **nomination-track** claim — no monetary limit, 1-working-day timeline, no affidavit/disclaimer/indemnity. The minor's status changes only **who receives** the money and **what representative documents** are needed.

---

### CS-MIN-001 — Sole minor nominee: payment to appointed payee, else guardian
- **Topic/Scenario:** Minor Nominee / depositor dies leaving a single nominee who is a minor
- **Rule statement:** The eligible balance is paid, on the minor nominee's behalf, to the **person appointed by the depositor** in the nomination to receive it; if no person was appointed, to the **guardian of the minor**, for the use of the minor.
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.4A(2)(a)–(b)** (India Code PDF p.4); **GSPR Rule 15(5)** (gazette p.36/PDF p.5); **R60(2)(vi)** (SB Order 31/2020 enclosure p.3)
- **SB Order:** 31/2020 · **GSPR:** 15(5), 14(2) · **CBS Manual:** Rule 60(2)(vi) · **POSB Manual:** Rule 87(2)(iv) (legacy, same effect)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Effective:** 01.04.2018 (Act) / 12.10.2018 (GSPR) / 28.08.2020 (R60)
- **Required Forms:** Form 11 (signed by appointee/guardian on the minor's behalf) · **Affidavit/Disclaimer/Indemnity:** None · **Court Order:** None (unless guardian is court-appointed)
- **Competent Authority / Limit:** any SPM/PM where nomination registered — **no limit**; 1 working day
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-002 — Appointment of the payee is made in the nomination itself
- **Scenario:** Depositor nominating a minor
- **Rule statement:** While nominating a minor, the depositor **shall appoint an individual** (Form 1 ¶17 / Form 10 appointment clause: "As the nominee(s) at Serial No.(s)… is/are minor(s), I appoint Shri/Smt/Kumari… to receive the sum due under the said account in the event of my death during the minority of the nominee(s)") — with the appointee's name, relationship and address, and the **minor nominee's date of birth** recorded in the nominee table.
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.4(3)**; **GSPR 14(2)**; Form 1 ¶17 & Form 10 (G.S.R. 1003(E), pp.43/51–52)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Effective:** 12.10.2018
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**
- **Note:** GSPR 14(2) is mandatory in form ("shall appoint") but CS-MIN-004/CS-MIN-001 confirm the nomination is **not invalid** without an appointee — payment simply routes to the guardian (ACT s.4A(2)(b) exists precisely for the no-appointee case).

### CS-MIN-003 — Minor nominee without any appointed payee: nomination fully valid
- **Scenario:** Nomination made in favour of a minor with no guardian/payee named
- **Rule statement:** The nomination remains valid; on the depositor's death payment is made to the **guardian of the minor for the use of the minor**.
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.4A(2)(b)**; **GSPR 15(5)** ("…and, if no such person has been appointed, to the guardian of the minor"); **R60(2)(vi)**
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Court Order:** None for natural guardian · **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-004 — Minor among multiple nominees / all nominees minors
- **Scenario:** (a) mix of major and minor nominees; (b) every nominee a minor
- **Rule statement:** Share mechanics are unchanged (GSPR 15(3)–(4); Topic 4). **Major nominees receive their shares directly; each minor nominee's share** is paid to the depositor's appointee (the Form 1/10 appointment clause covers the named minor serial numbers), else to that minor's guardian. Where **all** nominees are minors, every share is routed this way; the claim is presented by the appointee(s)/guardian(s) on the minors' behalf. No official text imposes any different limit, timeline or evidence for all-minor nominations.
- **Official Source:** GSPR 15(3)–(5); ACT s.4A(2)–(3); R60(2)(iv)–(vi), (xi)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Competent Authority / Limit:** nomination track — no limit
- **Cross References:** CS-MNM-003/004/007 (locked Topic 4)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-005 — Who is the guardian: statutory hierarchy; Muslim mother clarification
- **Scenario:** Identifying the person entitled to receive a minor nominee's money
- **Rule statement:** "Guardian", in relation to a minor, means — (i) **either of the parents**; (ii) where neither parent is alive or the only living parent is incapable of acting, a **person entitled under the law** in force to the care of the minor's property; (iii) a **legal guardian appointed by a court**. The **mother** of a **Muslim** minor is a valid guardian for deceased-claim settlement (irrespective of personal law), and payment to her is a valid discharge under ACT s.5.
- **Official Source:** **ACT s.3(h)**; **R60(7)(b) & Note 1** (SB Order 31/2020 enclosure p.9)
- **Applicable Schemes:** All · **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-006 — Natural guardian: no guardianship certificate; others: proof required
- **Scenario:** Proof of guardianship
- **Rule statement:** In **clear cases** where the **natural guardian** (father/mother) prefers the minor's claim, payment is made **without insisting on a Guardianship Certificate** (R60(6)(iii)). Where the claimant is **not** a guardian under the law applicable (e.g., relative caring for the minor), a **guardianship certificate** must be produced (R60(4)(A) Note 2(iii), stated for minor relatives of the deceased and applied by the department to minor-claimant situations generally). A **court-appointed guardian** proves status by the **court's guardianship order** (ACT s.3(h)(iii)).
- **Official Source:** R60(6)(iii); R60(4)(A) Note 2(iii); ACT s.3(h)
- **Required Documents:** natural guardian → own ID+address proof and relationship evidence on the claim; other/court guardians → guardianship certificate / court order + ID
- **Court Order Requirement:** only where guardianship itself is court-conferred (s.3(h)(iii)) or the person is not a natural guardian and obtains a certificate
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-007 — Declaration at payment: "minor is alive and the money is required for the minor"
- **Scenario:** Disbursement of the minor's share to appointee/guardian
- **Rule statement:** The person withdrawing on the minor's behalf shall **furnish a certificate that the minor is alive and that the money is required on behalf of the minor** (deceased-claim context: R60(7)(c); cognate certificates: GSPR 12(2) for withdrawals from minors' accounts and GSPR 20(2) for loan/withdrawal — "…is required for the use and welfare of… who is a minor… and is alive on this day").
- **Official Source:** **R60(7)(c)** (SB Order 31/2020); GSPR 12(2), 20(2) (declaration wording)
- **Required Declarations:** the above certificate; acquittance on the sanction/claim form
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-008 — Payment directly to a minor is not a valid discharge
- **Scenario:** Minor nominee (or minor claimant) seeks payment personally
- **Rule statement:** "The payment to a minor directly and not through a guardian **will not be a valid discharge** under Section 5 of the Act." Deceased-claim payments must therefore go to the appointee/guardian.
- **Boundary (not a nominee rule):** ACT **s.10** permits paying a **minor depositor's own deposit** to the minor personally *if the minor himself made the deposit* — this applies to the minor's own account, **not** to sums payable to a minor as nominee/claimant.
- **Official Source:** **R60(7) Note 2** (SB Order 31/2020 enclosure p.10); ACT s.5, s.10
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-009 — Guardian dies (or is changed) before settlement: succeeding guardian steps in
- **Scenario:** The guardian who would receive the minor nominee's money dies, or guardianship changes, before the claim is settled/paid
- **Rule statement:** The **succeeding guardian** — determined by the ACT s.3(h) hierarchy (surviving parent; else person entitled under law; else court-appointed guardian) or by **an order of a Court of Law in any case of change of guardian** — is entitled to act for the minor. Official anchor for guardian succession: **GSPR 10(4)** (as substituted by G.S.R. 488(E), 03.07.2023): "In the event of death of guardian or on order from any Court of Law in any case of change in the guardian, the succeeding guardian shall be eligible to operate the account of the minor…". The same succession logic governs receipt of a deceased-claim payment (the recipient must be *the guardian at the time of payment* — ACT s.4A(2)(b) present tense).
- **Official Source:** GSPR 10(4) (2023 text, via SB Order 16/2023); ACT s.3(h), s.4A(2)(b)
- **Required Documents:** death certificate of the former guardian (where relevant); succeeding guardian's ID; court order where guardianship is court-conferred/changed
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (succession principle official; its application to claim receipt is a direct statutory reading — noted in report)
- **Note:** If the **appointed payee** (14(2)) has died, payment routes to the guardian (CS-MIN-003; locked Topic 3 CS-PRE-007).

### CS-MIN-010 — Nominee attains majority before settlement: pays to the (former) minor directly
- **Scenario:** Nominee was a minor at nomination or at the depositor's death, but has **attained majority** by the time of settlement/payment
- **Rule statement:** The appointee's/guardian's authority exists only **"in the event of the death of the depositor during the minority of the nominee"** (ACT s.4(3); GSPR 14(2)) and payment is routed through them only **"where the nominee is a minor"** (ACT s.4A(2); GSPR 15(5) "Where the surviving nominee **is** a minor…") — assessed at payment. A nominee who **is no longer a minor** claims and receives payment **directly**, submitting his/her own identity documents (GSPR 15(7)) and date-of-birth evidence establishing majority.
- **Official Source:** ACT s.4(3), s.4A(2); GSPR 14(2), 15(5), 15(7) — construction of present-tense statutory text; no contrary official instruction exists
- **Required Documents:** nominee's own ID + address proof; DOB proof (the nomination itself records the minor nominee's DOB — Form 1 ¶17)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (statutory construction — flagged for explicit Directorate confirmation, **OQ-17**)

### CS-MIN-011 — Documents & identity requirements from the guardian/appointee (consolidated)
- **Scenario:** Any minor-nominee claim
- **Requirements:**
  - Form 11 / DoP claim form (in duplicate) signed by appointee/guardian for the minor's share; two witnesses with self-attested ID/address proofs (Addendum 16.09.2020).
  - Death certificate of depositor (and of any deceased nominee, in original).
  - Passbook/certificates in original.
  - **Appointee/guardian's ID + address proof** (R60(2)(viii)(e) "nominee(s) should be satisfactorily identified" applied to the recipient; GSPR 15(7); cognate GSPR 6(4): for accounts of minors "the identity documents of the guardian shall be furnished").
  - Guardianship certificate / court order **only** for non-natural guardians (CS-MIN-006).
  - "Minor alive + required for minor" certificate at disbursement (CS-MIN-007).
  - **No affidavit (13), disclaimer (14) or indemnity (15)** on the nomination track. (These arise only in **no-nomination** claims — where, additionally, a guardianship certificate is needed for **minor relatives/heirs** of the deceased if the claimant is not their legal guardian: R60(4)(A) Note 2(iii), locked Topic 2 CS-NON-004.)
- **Official Source:** as cited inline · **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-012 — Financial powers & monetary limits for every minor-nominee scenario
- **Rule statement:** The minor status of a nominee **does not alter sanctioning powers**:
  - Nomination in force (sole minor / mixed / all minors; incl. majority-attained and guardian-succession cases): **no monetary limit — any SPM/PM** of the office where the nomination is registered; GDS BPM accepts & forwards; 1 working day (R60(2) Note 1; 36/2020; 01/2023).
  - Minor **depositor** died without nomination (pre-2018 minor accounts, or nomination void): payment to guardian on claim + death certificate of the minor (R60(1) Note 2; GSPR 10(3); ACT s.4A(3A)) — treated as nomination-equivalent guardian payment, no limit stated; where instead **legal heirs of a deceased adult** claim and some heirs are minors, the **Topic 2 matrix** applies (₹50k/₹1L/₹5L; >₹5L legal evidence; 6 months).
  - Court orders: never required by the minor status itself — only via guardianship (court-appointed) or the general dispute/evidence rules.
- **Official Source:** R60(2) Note 1; R60(1) Note 2; SB Order 36/2020; GSPR 15(6)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED**

### CS-MIN-013 — Scheme-wise differences (minor nominee)
- **Rule statement:** The minor-nominee rules above apply **uniformly** to SB, RD, TD, MIS, SCSS, PPF, NSC, KVP and SSA — no scheme-specific deviation exists in any located official instrument. Scheme-flavoured notes:
  - **SB/MIS/PPF/SSA:** closure only; guardian receives the minor's share; interest per R60(10) table (scheme rate to death / POSA rate thereafter for SCSS & SSA; scheme-specific rows otherwise).
  - **RD/TD/NSC/KVP:** the continuation/transfer-to-own-name option (R60(10)–(11)) is **not officially addressed for a minor nominee** (a guardian can open/operate accounts on behalf of a minor under GSPR 4(1), and the sanction-memo text offers transfer "subject to the conditions laid down in the Rules governing the relevant Savings Scheme") — whether a guardian may elect continuation in the minor's name is **unregulated**; logged **OQ-18**; engine default = **closure with payment to appointee/guardian** unless the office accepts a guardian-operated transfer under scheme rules.
  - **SCSS:** minors cannot hold SCSS accounts and the spouse-continuation option presupposes an adult spouse **sole** nominee — a minor nominee therefore always takes by **closure** through guardian/appointee.
  - **SSA:** the account holder is a girl child; on **her** death the balance is paid to the **guardian** (account-holder side, GSPR 10(3)/SSA rules); a minor *nominee* on an SSA-linked claim follows the general rules above.
- **Official Source:** R60(10)–(11); GSPR 4(1), 10(3); SB Order 29/2021 (Finacle mechanics)
- **Verification Status:** **VERIFIED** (uniformity); OQ-18 logged; scheme-gazette re-check consolidated under OQ-12

## Superseded (never delete)

- Legacy **POSB Manual Vol I Rule 22(1)**: "No nomination shall be made in respect of an account opened by or on behalf of a minor" — superseded for post-01.04.2018 accounts by GSPR 14(5) (guardian **makes** nomination; guardian may nominate any individual including himself); pre-2018 minor accounts: no nomination, guardian payment (GSPR 14(5) proviso / ACT s.4A(3A)). Registered as **S-14**.
- Legacy Vol I 87(2)(iv) (minor nominee claims by appointed person/guardian under GSB Act s.4(3)) — continued in substance by R60(2)(vi) (S-4 covers the text replacement).

## Conflicts & gaps

- **No new hard conflicts.** Boundary clarified: ACT s.10 direct payment to minors applies to a minor's **own deposits**, never to nominee payments (R60(7) Note 2 controls).
- **OQ-17:** minor-attains-majority-before-payment — statutory construction is unambiguous but no express DoP circular; seek confirmation.
- **OQ-18:** continuation of RD/TD/NSC/KVP in a **minor nominee's** name via guardian — unregulated.
- **OQ-19:** where the depositor appointed a payee (14(2)) and the **guardian objects/competes** with the appointee — no official precedence text beyond the statute's order (appointee first: s.4A(2)(a)); engine follows statutory order.
